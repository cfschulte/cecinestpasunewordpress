The latest news of our good ole USofA is that there was yet another mass shooting. This one in Parkland, Florida. That makes 30 this year. It is mid February. There is not much that I can say that hasn't been said before.

We are at a point where rational discourse is not an option. We must get enough people to simply say to the gun rights advocates, "that's it! Your opinion no longer matters." Then change the laws. Given the way our electorate works, I don't think that's possible through the normal democratic process. I think we need to find a way to make lawsuits happen.

Bill Clinton was able to fight tobacco in the `90s with lawsuits. The lobbies were too powerful and the lawmakers just wouldn't let that happen. Class action lawsuits started to put financial pressure on the right groups and things changed. It would be nice if we could sue the lawmakers directly, but my attorney brother-in-law told me there are reasons you can't - something about whether or not the government can hire a third party to take the responsibility. That's why you can sue the state if you're hurt on an unsafe road.

But why can't you sue the NRA? Whenever something like this happens, the families of the slain should given multi-million dollar payouts and the living victims should be able to sue for the trauma they suffered.
